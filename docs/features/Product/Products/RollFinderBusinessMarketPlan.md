# RollFinder Business Market Plan

Version: 1.0

Product: RollFinder

Market: United Kingdom

Launch City: London

Stage: MVP / Market Validation

---

# Executive Summary

RollFinder is a discovery platform for Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu practitioners who need to quickly find academies, drop-in classes, and open mats.

The MVP focuses on London and solves a narrow, high-frequency problem:

> Help grapplers find somewhere to train in under 30 seconds.

The business opportunity is to become the default discovery layer for BJJ training in the UK, starting with London. The first commercial goal is not to build a full academy management system, but to validate search demand, academy listing demand, and willingness to pay for visibility.

---

# Market Opportunity

## Primary Market

The initial market is Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu practitioners in London, including:

* Local hobbyists looking for open mats
* Competitors looking for extra rounds
* Travelers visiting London
* Students moving between academies
* Beginners searching for nearby gyms

## Secondary Market

The secondary market is academy owners who want:

* More visitor traffic
* Better local search visibility
* Easier promotion of open mats
* A simple public profile without maintaining another full website
* A low-cost marketing channel focused specifically on BJJ users

## Why London First

London is the best launch market because it has:

* A dense concentration of BJJ academies
* High population mobility
* Many visiting practitioners
* Strong public transport coverage
* A mix of independent and affiliated academies
* Enough academy density to make search and map discovery useful immediately

Need:

* Academy websites vary widely in quality

* Searchable BJJ-specific directory
* Upcoming event listings
* Simple filters by city, postcode, academy, and gi type

## User Segment 3: Academy Owners

Need:

* Increase visibility
* Attract visitors and beginners
* Promote open mats
* Control listing accuracy

Pain points:

* Social media posts disappear quickly
* Google listings do not highlight BJJ-specific information
* Maintaining website event pages takes effort

RollFinder value:

* Claimable academy profile
* Open mat publishing
* Qualified traffic from people already looking to train

## Direct Alternatives

* Google Maps
* Academy websites
* Instagram pages
* Facebook groups
* WhatsApp communities
* Generic sports or fitness directories

## Competitive Gaps

Most alternatives are not purpose-built for BJJ discovery.

Common gaps include:

* No reliable open mat listings
* No gi/no-gi distinction
* No consistent drop-in information
* No central directory for visitors
* No easy way for academies to promote recurring sessions

RollFinder can win by being simpler, more specific, and more current than generic tools.

The product should avoid becoming a broad social network during the MVP stage. The advantage is speed and clarity.

# Go-To-Market Strategy

## Phase 1: Data Foundation

Goal:

Build the most useful London BJJ directory before aggressive marketing.

Actions:

* Seed 50 London academies
* Add accurate addresses, websites, and contact details
* Add 100 open mat events
* Verify academy pages manually
* Prioritize academies with known drop-in or open mat activity

Success metrics:

* 90% of seeded academy profiles have complete location data
* 50+ upcoming open mat listings
* Search and map pages are useful without user-generated content

## Phase 2: Practitioner Acquisition

Goal:

Drive BJJ practitioners to search and bookmark RollFinder.

Channels:

* Instagram reels and stories
* Reddit BJJ and London communities
* Local BJJ Facebook groups
* WhatsApp group sharing
* Posters or QR cards at open mats
* SEO landing pages for London boroughs and postcodes

Campaign ideas:

* "Find a London open mat this weekend"
* "Training while visiting London?"
* "No-gi open mats near you"
* "BJJ gyms near [postcode]"

Success metrics:

* 1,000 monthly visitors
* 30% returning visitor rate
* 500 academy search sessions per month
* 100 map interactions per month

## Phase 3: Academy Owner Outreach

Goal:

Get academy owners to claim listings and submit open mats.

Actions:

* Email academy owners with their existing listing
* Offer free profile updates during the launch period
* Ask owners to confirm open mat schedules
* Provide a simple "Listed on RollFinder" badge
* Prioritize academies already hosting open mats

Outreach message:

> We added your academy to RollFinder, a London BJJ directory for people looking for academies and open mats. You can claim your listing for free and update your details.

Success metrics:

* 20 claimed academy profiles
* 30 owner-submitted updates
* 25 active open mat listings maintained by academies

## Phase 4: Monetization Pilot

Goal:

Validate academy willingness to pay for visibility.

Initial paid offers:

* Promoted academy listing
* Featured open mat placement
* Sponsored borough page
* Premium academy profile

Suggested pilot pricing:

* Featured open mat: £10-£25 per week
* Promoted academy listing: £49-£99 per month
* Premium profile: £19-£49 per month

Pilot rules:

* Keep pricing simple
* Limit promoted inventory
* Track clicks, profile views, and direction taps
* Offer founding academy discounts

Success metrics:

* 5 paying academies
* £250+ monthly recurring revenue
* 30% conversion from contacted claimed academies to paid pilot

# Monetization Strategy

## Revenue Stream 1: Promoted Listings

Academies pay to appear higher in search results or featured sections.

Best for:

* Academies seeking new students
* Academies in competitive boroughs
* New academies launching in London

## Revenue Stream 2: Featured Open Mats

Academies pay to feature specific open mat sessions.

Best for:

* Weekend open mats
* Competition preparation sessions
* Special guest sessions
* Charity open mats

## Revenue Stream 3: Premium Academy Profiles

Academies pay for enhanced profile features.

Possible premium features:

* More photos
* Prominent call-to-action buttons
* Drop-in policy section
* Class timetable
* Featured coach information
* Analytics dashboard

## Revenue Stream 4: Sponsored Local Pages

Academies or brands sponsor borough-level pages.

Examples:

* "BJJ in Shoreditch"
* "Open mats in South London"
* "No-gi training in East London"

* Qualified visitor intent
* Low cost compared with broad advertising
* Easy setup
* Local search visibility

# Risks and Mitigation

## Risk: Inaccurate Listings

Mitigation:

* Let academies claim profiles
* Add clear update request flow
* Review open mat dates regularly
* Prioritize verified listings

## Risk: Low Academy Adoption

Mitigation:

* Keep claiming free
* Show owners profile traffic
* Start with open mat hosts
* Offer founding academy discounts

## Risk: Low User Retention

Mitigation:

* Keep open mat data fresh
* Add weekly roundups
* Improve postcode and map search
* Focus on weekend training use cases

## Risk: Monetization Too Early

Mitigation:

* Do not charge for basic listings
* Pilot paid offers with a small group
* Tie pricing to measurable visibility
* Keep paid placements transparent

# Strategic Focus

RollFinder should stay narrow during the MVP phase.

Build:

* Discovery
* Search
* Map
* Claiming
* Open mat promotion
* Admin management

Avoid:

* Reviews
* Ratings
* Social feeds
* Messaging
* Membership billing
* Academy management tools

The market plan succeeds if RollFinder becomes the trusted place to answer one question:

> Where can I train BJJ today?
